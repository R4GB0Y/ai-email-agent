# Your AI Email Assistant — What It Does and How to Turn It Off

---

## What this is

Every 15 minutes, this assistant reads your Gmail inbox, figures out which emails actually need your attention, and sends you a summary in Slack — along with a draft reply for anything that needs one.

You stay in your inbox. Nothing changes in your email. The assistant never writes or sends anything on your behalf.

---

## What happens every 15 minutes

1. It reads your unread emails (the same ones you see when you open Gmail)
2. It skips newsletters, receipts, and spam automatically
3. For the emails that remain, it figures out: *what category is this? how urgent? does it need a reply?*
4. It sends you a summary in Slack, sorted from most urgent to least

That's it. No emails are moved, labeled, deleted, or sent.

**What the colors mean:**
- 🔴 Red — needs your attention today
- 🟡 Yellow — needs attention this week
- 🟢 Green — for your information, no action needed

**What `[DECISION NEEDED]` means in a draft:**  
The assistant deliberately leaves these markers when a reply requires a choice only you can make (approve or decline, accept or reject). It will never fill in that choice for you.

## What it will never do

- **Never send an email.** Drafts appear in Slack as suggestions. Nothing is sent until you copy the text, paste it into Gmail, and click Send yourself.
- **Never delete an email.** It cannot delete, archive, or move anything.
- **Never mark emails as read.** Your inbox stays exactly as it is.
- **Never reply on your behalf.** The assistant has read-only access to Gmail. It is technically incapable of sending or modifying anything.
- **Never store your emails.** Email content is sent to the AI model to generate the summary and draft, then immediately discarded. Nothing is saved to a database.
- **Never access attachments.** It reads the email subject and body text only.

## How the assistant got access to your Gmail

You (or someone on your behalf) went through Google's authorization screen and clicked "Allow." That gave the assistant permission to **read** your Gmail — and only read it.

The permission is scoped to `gmail.readonly`. That scope cannot send email, delete email, or access Google Drive, Calendar, or any other Google service.

You can verify this at any time:

1. Go to **myaccount.google.com/permissions**
2. Find the app named **ai-email-agent** (or the name it was registered under)
3. You will see "Read all resources and their metadata in Gmail" — this is the read-only permission

## How to turn it off

**Option 1 — Revoke Gmail access (immediate)**

1. Go to [myaccount.google.com/permissions](https://myaccount.google.com/permissions)
2. Find the app and click **Remove Access**

The assistant will stop being able to read your email immediately. The next time it tries to run, it will fail with an authorization error and stop.

**Option 2 — Stop the process**

If you have access to where the assistant is running, press Ctrl-C in the terminal or stop the Docker container. It will not run again until someone starts it.

**Option 3 — Ask the person who set it up**

They can stop the scheduler or change the schedule interval to never run.

## Frequently asked questions

**Does the assistant read every email in my inbox?**

It reads up to 20 unread emails per run (the default). Newsletters, receipts, and spam are automatically dropped before anything reaches the Slack digest. Emails already marked as read are not processed.

**Can I change how the drafts sound?**

Yes. Ask the person who set it up to change the `RESPONSE_TONE` setting. Options are `formal` (default — professional language), `casual` (friendly, contractions), or `brief` (two or three sentences, no greeting).

**What if the draft says something wrong?**

The draft is a starting point, not a final answer. Every Slack message includes the footer: *"Drafts are suggestions, review before sending."* Anything sensitive (approvals, commitments, numbers) will be marked `[DECISION NEEDED]` and left blank for you to fill in.

**Can it access email from before today?**

It only processes emails that are currently unread in your inbox. It does not have access to your sent mail, read mail, archived mail, or any historical email.

**Who can see the Slack messages?**

The digest goes to whichever Slack channel or person the webhook is pointed at. The people in that channel can see your email summaries and draft previews. If that channel includes others, they will see the digests too.
